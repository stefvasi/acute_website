    <article class="proj-card">
      <div class="proj-card__header">
        <span class="proj-card__num">01</span>
        <div>
          <h2 class="proj-card__title">VibroSleeve</h2>
          <span class="proj-card__tag proj-card__tag--active">Active</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Haptics</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p>A forearm-worn vibrotactile display with six voice-coil actuators arranged in a 2&times;3 array, designed to deliver Braille-like spatial patterns through the skin. The device serves as the shared hardware platform across several ACUTE projects.</p>
          <p>Sequential presentation of patterns achieves <strong>93% recognition accuracy</strong> versus 26% with simultaneous stimulation. At optimal interstimulus intervals (300 ms), short patterns reach 92&ndash;98% accuracy. The system has been validated with 14 distinct patterns and tested for both frequency and location effects on the forearm.</p>

    <article class="proj-card">
      <div class="proj-card__header">
        <span class="proj-card__num">02</span>
        <div>
          <h2 class="proj-card__title">Prosthetic Haptic Feedback</h2>
          <span class="proj-card__tag proj-card__tag--active">Active</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Haptics</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Prosthetics</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p>A vibrotactile feedback system that conveys prosthetic knee-joint position to transfemoral amputees without requiring visual monitoring. Forearm-mounted actuators encode four distinct joint angles through sequential tactile patterns.</p>
          <p>Healthy participants achieved <strong>72.4% accuracy</strong> with sequential cues &mdash; nearly double the 42.7% with simultaneous presentation. Accuracy reached a ceiling of ~93% at 240 ms signal duration. A comprehensive review of lower-limb haptic feedback systems identified standardized evaluation protocols as the key gap in the field.</p>
        </div>

    <!-- Haptic Harmony -->
    <article class="proj-card">
      <div class="proj-card__header">
        <span class="proj-card__num">03</span>
        <div>
          <h2 class="proj-card__title">Haptic Harmony</h2>
          <span class="proj-card__tag proj-card__tag--active">Active</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Haptics</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Perception</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p>Music-to-tactile translation via wearable vibrotactile devices, enabling richer musical experience for cochlear implant users. The project investigates how amplitude and frequency map to tactile discriminability, establishing the perceptual parameters for effective haptic encoding of musical content.</p>
          <p>A three-actuator sleeve delivering tactile accompaniment to music enhanced perception beyond what a cochlear implant alone provides. Separate psychophysical work showed that <strong>amplitude is the dominant channel</strong> for vibrotactile discrimination at the wrist &mdash; frequency-only changes were near chance, and combined cues offered no additional benefit.</p>

    <!-- Viking HRTF Dataset -->
    <article class="proj-card">
      <div class="proj-card__header">
        <span class="proj-card__num">04</span>
        <div>
          <h2 class="proj-card__title">Viking HRTF Dataset</h2>
          <span class="proj-card__tag proj-card__tag--completed">Completed</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Acoustics</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p>An open, full-sphere Head-Related Transfer Function dataset measured at <strong>1,513 spatial positions</strong> on a KEMAR mannequin fitted with 20 custom-molded silicone pinnae. The dataset enables controlled study of how pinna shape influences spatial hearing cues, and underpins machine learning efforts to predict personalised HRTFs.</p>
          <p>Synthetic pinnae manufactured with 0.25 mm scanner accuracy allow isolation of individual anthropometric effects &mdash; something impossible with natural ears. An MLP trained on 15 anthropometric parameters achieved <strong>3.54% mean prediction error</strong> for HRTFs (vs. ~15% with a standard KEMAR). A separate model predicting the lowest pinna spectral notch from 3D meshes achieved <strong>3.3% median mismatch</strong>, halving prior methods.</p>
        </div>

          <span class="proj-card__tag proj-card__tag--active">Active</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Acoustics</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p><strong>Perceptual Aspects of Acoustics for Active Auditory Channels</strong> &mdash; investigating whether geometrical acoustics simulations can faithfully preserve the auditory cues essential for human echolocation, enabling virtual training environments for blind echolocators.</p>
          <p>Validated against the BRAS benchmark dataset, the work establishes requirements for virtual training rooms that accurately reproduce echo-based spatial information. The goal is to let blind individuals practise echolocation safely in simulation before transferring skills to the real world.</p>

      <div class="proj-card__header">
        <span class="proj-card__num">06</span>
        <div>
          <h2 class="proj-card__title">Room Acoustics Model Reduction</h2>
          <span class="proj-card__tag proj-card__tag--active">Active</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Acoustics</span>
          <span class="proj-card__tag">Simulation</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p>Structure-preserving model-order reduction for time-domain room acoustics based on the Linearized Euler Equations. The approach maintains numerical stability at complex frequency-dependent boundaries while dramatically reducing computation time.</p>
          <p>The new full-order formulation achieves a <strong>3.5&times; speedup</strong> over the conventional approach. When combined with the reduced-order model, total speedup reaches <strong>100&times;</strong> &mdash; making wave-based acoustic simulation practical for interactive applications and room design.</p>
        </div>

          <span class="proj-card__tag">Haptics</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="proj-card__body">
        <div class="proj-card__desc">
          <p>Exploring low-cognitive-load non-verbal communication through rhythmic vibrotactile patterns. The project investigates the psychophysics of temporal haptic encoding &mdash; how rhythm, timing, and intensity sequences can convey simple messages through wearable devices without demanding the user's visual or auditory attention.</p>
          <p>Building on ACUTE's established findings that sequential presentation and amplitude variation are the most effective vibrotactile channels, this project extends the work toward practical everyday communication scenarios.</p>
